Lesson 1 : The Meaning of Independence Day

"In this lesson, we will explore the significance of Independence Day, its historical context, and how it is celebrated."

Reading:

The Declaration of Independence

Independence Day celebration

On July 4, 1776, a group of Americans representing the thirteen British colonies on the Atlantic coast of North America signed a document stating the right for the colonies to be free and independent from Britain. This document is known as the Declaration of Independence. Independence Day is celebrated on July 4 because that is the day when the Continental Congress approved it. But as for Britain, it did not recognize this fact until 1783, when the American colonists won a victory in the war of independence with Britain. The Declaration of Independence was signed in 1776 in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, in a building now known as Independence Hall. In the glass-enclosed pavilion near Independence Hall is the famous Liberty Bell which rang to mark the occasion of the adoption of the Declaration and to tell the people in the streets that a new country had been born. The cracked Liberty Bell is a symbol of America's struggle for free-dom. Just as a man's facial lines and creases are a visible sign of the stress and strain he has survived, so the crack in the Liberty Bell serves to remind Americans that their forefathers did not win liberty for their country and its people without strain and stress. John Adams, a lawyer, the first Vice President and the second President of the United States, was one of the first men to sign the Declaration of Inde-pendence. He wrote to his wife, "I believe that it will be celebrated by succeeding generations as the great anniversary festival..." He accurately predicted the later Independence Day celebrations, and may have even started the tradition with these words. Every July 4, Americans have a holiday from work. Some of them have day-long picnics, others entertain themselves with a friendly baseball game or a pie-eating or water-melon-eating contest. Some cities have parades with people dressed as original founding fathers who march to the music of high school bands. At dusk, people in towns and cities gather to watch the fireworks display. Wherever Americans are around the globe, they will gather for a traditional 4th of July celebration!

    Vocabulary Flashcards

    1. the occasion of adoption

    Definition: The event or ceremony where something is officially accepted.
    қабылдау оқиғасы

    2. facial lines and creases

    Definition: Wrinkles or folds that appear on a person's face, usually due to aging or expression.
    беттегі әжімдер мен бүктемелер

    3. sign of stress and strain

    Definition: A visible indication of tension or pressure on someone's appearance or behavior.
    стресстің және ауыртпалықтың белгісі

    4. succeeding generations

    Definition: Future groups of people born after the current generation.
    келе жатқан ұрпақтар

    5. anniversary festival

    Definition: A celebration marking the yearly recurrence of a significant event.
    мерейтойлық фестиваль

    6. to predict celebrations

    Definition: To foresee or anticipate festivities or joyful gatherings.
    мерекелерді болжау

    7. to entertain oneself

    Definition: To engage in enjoyable activities for personal amusement.
    өзін-өзі көңіл көтеру

    8. contest

    Definition: A competition or challenge where participants strive to win.
    бәсеке, конкурс

    9. to march to music

    Definition: To walk in a formal or organized way, usually in step with music.
    музыкаға сәйкестендіріп жүріп өту

    10. school band

    Definition: A group of students who play musical instruments together, often as part of school activities.
    мектеп оркестрі

    Grammar :

    Common English Tenses:

    1. Present Simple

    Formula: Subject + base form of the verb (add "s/es" for he/she/it)

    Example: She works at a bank.

    2. Present Continuous

    Formula: Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing

    Example: They are studying for their exams.

    3. Past Simple

    Formula: Subject + past form of the verb

    Example: He visited his grandmother.

    4. Past Continuous

    Formula: Subject + was/were + verb-ing

    Example: I was reading a book.

    5. Future Simple

    Formula: Subject + will + base form of the verb

    Example: We will travel to France.

    6. Future Continuous

    Formula: Subject + will be + verb-ing

    Example: She will be working late tonight.

    7. Present Perfect

    Formula: Subject + has/have + past participle

    Example: They have finished their homework.

    8. Past Perfect

    Formula: Subject + had + past participle

    Example: He had left before I arrived.

    9. Future Perfect

    Formula: Subject + will have + past participle

    Example: They will have completed the project by next week.

    Writing Tasks: The Meaning of Independence Day

    Task 1: The Significance of July 4

    Objective: Write about why July 4 is celebrated as Independence Day in the United States.

    Instructions:

    • Describe the historical events that led to the signing of the Declaration of Independence.
    • Explain the role of the Continental Congress in approving the Declaration on July 4, 1776.
    • Discuss why this date became a symbol of freedom and liberty for Americans.
    • Include examples of how Independence Day is celebrated in modern times.

    Length: 100-150 words

    Task 2: The Liberty Bell: A Symbol of Freedom

    Objective: Write an essay discussing the significance of the Liberty Bell as a symbol of American independence.

    Instructions:

    • Explain the history of the Liberty Bell and its connection to the Declaration of Independence.
    • Discuss how the crack in the bell has become a symbol of the struggles faced during the fight for freedom.
    • Provide examples of how the Liberty Bell continues to inspire Americans today.

    Length: 100-150 words
